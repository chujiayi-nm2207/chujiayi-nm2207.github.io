A glimpse into Singapore’s working culture

This is Jayde.

After 1.5 years in University and serving an internship, Jayde is unsure if what she is doing is her passion and if she wants to pursue this career. However, she feels that it is too late to change her field of studies.

Does Jayde sound like you?

Let's take a look at job satisfaction around the world.

Job Satisfaction Percentages Around The World

However, a study has shown that Singapore has the worst mental health amongst Southeast Asia, with employees having the lowest levels of engagement, job satisfaction and overall quality of life.

Only 1 in 3 Singaporeans said they ‘always’ or ‘nearly always’ look forward to going to work daily.

Only 3 in 5 Singaporeans said that they are motivated to go to work every day.

49% of Singaporeans are satisfied with their current job, which is below the global average of 62%.

Despite the low job satisfaction, why are people still in their jobs?

58% of employees desire to build a better life for themselves and their loved ones, while 53% hope to achieve a sense of accomplishment through their work. 50% hope to earn more income and 30% of those aged between 16-24 fear not being able to do as well as their peers. Contrary to popular belief, salary is not the main key driver of satisfaction among Singaporean employees.

However, employees fear switching careers because...

1. Lack of Loyalty

In highly bureaucratic environments, loyalty and years of service is of importance. Hence, those who job hop may be perceived as someone who does not possess loyalty.


2. Inability to Adapt

Elder employers may also associate job hopping as not taking one’s career seriously and the inability to adapt to new working environments and cultures.

Nonetheless, these are main factors why employees leave their companies

according to a survey conducted among 500 Singaporean jobseekers

Before Covid-19

Employees who were dissatisfied with their jobs chose to stay with it as they fear having gaps in their CV and being labelled as a “Job Hopper”. (Lim, 2022)



During Covid-19

Companies are more reluctant to hire those who looked like job hoppers as they did not want to invest in those who might soon leave for a new job again.(Lim, 2022)



After Covid-19

Employers realised that having such judgements are to no one’s interest. By enquiring deeper into someone’s reason for job hopping might provide them with valuable insights. (Lim, 2022)

While more employers are now more accepting of employees who have made significant career changes in the span of two years, the hiring process will be made stringent to determine if the person is worth investing in.

Alternatively...

Here’s how you can positively portray yourself to potential employers who are more receptive to changes in career as in these fluid times, restructuring can be mandatory for success.

1. Change Is Mandatory

In certain industries, it is the culture to change firms frequently. Such changes in career can be linked to growth and exposure and staying too long might be associated with lack of exposure or disability to obtain better offers.




3. Evolution

The original job scope may no longer be available due to technology advancements, digitalisation, sector transformation and company restructuring. Covid-19 has also led to the increase of micro-entrepreneurship and home businesses.


2. Introducing Diversity

Job hoppers can introduce diversity and alternative perspectives to the company. Changes in career can be linked with adaptability and the ability to learn fast in a foreign environment.






4. Exploration & Discovery

In the initial stages of career, employers are more receptive and forgiving to the idea of changes in career as such changes can be associated with attempts of self-discovery to find what individuals want and do not want.
